What am I looking at?

A Poincaré section records a trajectory only at the instants it punches through a chosen slice of phase space (here y=0 with py>0 for the flow). A quasi-periodic orbit lives on an invariant torus, so its punctures fall on a smooth closed curve. Chaos scatters punctures into a haze.

The KAM theorem

Write a near-integrable system as H = H₀ + λH₁. At λ=0 every orbit sits on a torus. Kolmogorov, Arnold and Moser proved that for small λ most of these tori merely deform and survive — specifically the ones whose frequency vector ω is Diophantine, |⟨k,ω⟩| ≥ κ/|k|ᵗ. The "most irrational" tori are the last to break.

Birkhoff & stability

Near such a torus the dynamics can be put into Birkhoff normal form. Eliasson, Fayad & Krikorian (Duke Math. J., 2015) showed a Diophantine torus is never isolated: if its normal form is non-degenerate it is accumulated by a positive-measure cloud of KAM tori — exactly the colored nests you see persisting on the left.

…and the neural net

The companion notebook learns this same flow with a Hamiltonian neural network: it predicts a scalar energy and moves along its symplectic gradient, so energy is conserved by construction and the learned trajectories stay pinned to the tori. A plain network, lacking that physics, drifts off them.
